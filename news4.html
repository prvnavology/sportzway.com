Clubs to Avoid at the Start of 2024/25 Fantasy Premier League

The Scout explains why early interest in Brentford, Arsenal, and Wolves assets may be limited

Arsenal

The Gunners were among the most reliable sides in Fantasy last season, excelling at both ends of the pitch. Mikel Arteta’s men recorded a league-high 18 clean sheets, with Ben White, William Saliba, and Gabriel topping the defender standings. Additionally, four of Arsenal’s midfielders – Bukayo Saka, Martin Odegaard, Kai Havertz, and Declan Rice – scored over 160 points each. However, their early fixtures are daunting, likely reducing the appeal of their players for initial squads. Arsenal will face three of last season’s top five teams – Aston Villa, Tottenham Hotspur, and Manchester City – in their first five matches, each scoring at least four in the FDR. On the plus side, Arsenal will also host Wolverhampton Wanderers and Brighton & Hove Albion during this period. Nonetheless, many managers may opt for players from other clubs with more favorable early schedules.

Brentford

The Bees have a similarly challenging start, with three tough away games paired with two more manageable home fixtures. While Brentford will play Crystal Palace and Southampton at home in the first five Gameweeks, they will also travel to face three of last season’s top five – Liverpool, Manchester City, and Spurs. As a result, Brentford’s key attackers, Bryan Mbeumo and Ivan Toney, might be overlooked by many managers until their schedule improves in Gameweek 6. From that point, the Bees will have three favorable home matches in the next four Gameweeks against West Ham United, Wolves, and Ipswich Town.

Wolves

Wolves face a brutal start to the season, making their players likely candidates to be avoided until late October. Gary O’Neil’s team will go up against six of last season’s top seven sides in their first eight fixtures. This tough run begins with an away game at Arsenal and concludes with a home match against Manchester City in Gameweek 8. However, Wolves’ fixtures improve significantly from Gameweek 9-17, with seven of their nine matches scoring just two in the FDR. During this period, Rayan Ait-Nouri stands out as a solid defensive option, while Pedro Neto, Hwang Hee-chan, and Matheus Cunha could offer excellent value in attack.

Wolves’ First Eight Matches

  • Arsenal (A)
  • Aston Villa (H)
  • Chelsea (A)
  • Tottenham Hotspur (H)
  • Liverpool (A)
  • Manchester United (H)
  • Newcastle United (A)
  • Manchester City (H)
